Representation · Contract analysis

Read every clause before you sign.

A first professional contract is the moment a young player has the least leverage and the most to lose. We review every clause in plain language — term, wages, image rights, release, solidarity — and flag what needs to change before anyone signs.

First professional · CPL · 6 clauses

§ 4.2

Term & option years

Two-year term with two club-side option years. Standard, but the options are unilateral — the club controls four years total.

Clear
§ 5.1

Wage structure & bonuses

Base wage in line with league scale for the age band. Appearance bonuses defined and reasonable.

Clear
§ 6.4

Image rights

Club claims 100% of commercial image rights with no carve-out for the player's personal sponsors. Needs negotiation.

Review
§ 7.2

Release / buyout clause

No fixed release clause. For a player on this trajectory, a defined buyout protects future mobility — recommend adding one.

Review
§ 8.1

Performance bonuses

Promotion and call-up bonuses clearly defined. Achievable and well-structured.

Clear
§ 9.5

Solidarity contribution

Clause waives the player's former club solidarity entitlement under FIFA RSTP. This is non-standard and disadvantages the player's development chain. Flag for removal.

How it works

Three passes before a signature.

01

Plain-language read

Every clause translated out of legal language so the player and family understand exactly what they're agreeing to.

02

Flag & benchmark

Each term checked against league scale, age band, and FIFA regulations. Anything below standard gets flagged with a reason.

03

Negotiate the changes

We take the flagged clauses back to the club and negotiate them before signature — from the player's side of the table.
